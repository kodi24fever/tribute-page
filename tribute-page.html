    Here's a time line of Martin Luther King life:

  • 1929 - Born in Atlanta, Gerogia.
  • 1942 - Became the youngest assistant manager of a newspaper delivery station for the Atlanta Journal.
  • 1948 - Graduated at the age of 19 from Morehouse with a B.A. in sociology.
  • 1953 - Married Correta Scott on the lawn of her parents' house in her hometown of Heiberger, Alabama.
  • 1955 - Recieved his Ph.D degree in in systematic theology at Boston University.
  • 1957 - Founded the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC).
  • 1958 - Signed copies of his book Stride Toward Freedom in Blumstein's department store in Harlem.
  • 1962 - Went to jail.
  • 1964 - King and the SCLC joined forces with Robert Hayling's then-controversial movement in St. Augustine, Florida.
  • 1963 - Organized the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom.
  • 1964 - King became the youngest winner of the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1968 - Was assasinated.
